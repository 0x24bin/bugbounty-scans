Fundraising and Donations on Facebook

Learn more about how your charitable organization can accept donations and let supporters create fundraisers on Facebook.

Getting Started
How does my organization qualify to use Facebook's fundraising tools?
Facebook's fundraising and donation tools are only available for charitable organizations based in the United States or these European countries. To qualify for Facebook fundraising and donation tools, a charitable organization will be required to meet the following criteria:
  • U.S.-based charities must be a 501(c)(3) organization, registered with the IRS, have a tax ID number and have a bank account registered with a licensed financial services institution.
  • Charitable organizations based in the European countries where Facebook donation and fundraising tools are available must provide a VAT number (if applicable), charity registration number (where applicable), a charity registration document issued or approved by a government entity and a bank account registered with a licensed financial services institution.
If the charitable organization meets these criteria, follow these steps to be able to accept donations on Facebook:
  1. Make sure that the charity's Facebook Page category is 'Nonprofit Organization’ or 'Charity Organization' and that its address is included in the ‘About’ section of its Facebook Page.
  2. Sign up for Facebook Payments from a Page admin account.
  3. Accept Facebook's terms of service.
  4. Accept the terms of Business Manager (for organizations that use Business Manager)
Note: If your charity is using Business Manager, please sign up to accept donations on Facebook here: https://business.facebook.com/donate/signup.
A charitable organization will also be required to provide the following information during the sign-up process for Facebook payments:
  • Date of birth and address of the charity's CEO or executive director.
  • Bank account details including bank name, bank account holder's name (organization's name), a legible and official bank letter or statement dated within the last 3 months, SWIFT Code and bank IBAN number.
  • Charity registration documents if the charitable organization is based in France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Poland, Netherlands, Belgium, Sweden, Portugal, Denmark, Norway, Austria, Finland and Luxembourg.

How do I allow people to fundraise on Facebook for my organization?
To allow fundraising for your nonprofit on Facebook:
  1. Sign up to accept donations on Facebook.
  2. Clicking Publishing Tools in the top menu of your Facebook Page.
  3. Click Donation Settings under Fundraisers.
  4. Check the box next to Allow people to create fundraisers
  5. Check the box next to Allow people to add donate buttons next to their posts.
  6. Click Save.

How do I add a donate button to my charity's Facebook Page?
To add a donate button to your charitable organization's Facebook Page:
  1. Go to the charity's Page (you must be a Page admin).
  2. Click Add a button.
  3. Select Donate through a website to send people to the charity's website to donate or select Donate through Facebook to allow people to donate directly through Facebook.
Note: The charitable organization must be signed up for Facebook Payments to allow people to donate directly through Facebook.

Donations, Payouts and Fees
Is there a fee for donations made on Facebook?
Note: All fees on donations made to European charities are currently being waived.
Donations to US-based Charitable Organizations
Donations to US-based charitable organizations that have been approved to receive donations through Facebook Payments are assessed a 5% fee. Two percent covers costs of nonprofit vetting, fraud prevention, operational costs and payment support. The remaining 3% covers payment processing.
Donations to US-based charitable organizations that receive funds through the Network for Good's Donor Advised Fund, are assessed a 5.75% fee. Of this fee, 3.5% covers credit card processing, payment support and fraud prevention. The other portion of the fee (2.25%) is deducted by the Network for Good's Donor Advised Fund to cover charity vetting and support, donation disbursement and operations.
Donations to Charitable Organizations based in Europe
Donations to charitable organizations based in these countries in Europe where Facebook fundraising and donation tools are available are assessed a 5% fee. Of this fee, 3.5% covers the costs of nonprofit vetting, fraud protection, operational costs and payment support. The remaining 1.5% covers payment processing.
Donations to Personal Fundraisers
For each donation made to a personal cause on Facebook, 6.9% + $.30 of the donation will go to payment processing fees, fundraiser vetting and fraud prevention.
Donations to Charitable Organizations Through Linked Campaigns
If a donation is made to a Facebook fundraiser that's linked to a campaign on a nonprofit's website, the fees charged will depend on the nonprofit's payment processor.

How do charities receive donations made on Facebook?
Charities Registered with Facebook Payments
When a charitable organization that is registered with Facebook Payments has reached the minimum donation payout amount, it becomes eligible for a payout, which occurs on a bi-weekly basis. The payout is a wire transfer (ACH/direct deposit) to the organization’s bank account. If a charitable organization's total donations hasn't reached the minimum payout amount of $100 USD, the funds will roll over until the amount reaches the minimum amount and becomes eligible for the bi-weekly payout.
Payouts generally take 2 weeks to process. Funds donated between the 1st and 15th of a month will likely be paid out on the first day of the following month. Funds donated between the 16th and the end of the month will likely be paid on the 16th of the following month.
Charities Not Registered with Facebook Payments (US only)
If the charitable organization isn't registered with Facebook Payments, any donations received are distributed through the Network for Good's Donor Advised Fund. Donations are generally distributed a month and a half after the last date of the month in which the donations were made. For example, a donation made in January will be sent to the nonprofit on March 15.
Fundraiser Linked to a Charity's Website
If a donation is made to a Facebook fundraiser linked to a campaign on a nonprofit's website, the donation is distributed through the charitable organization's payment processor.

What's Network for Good's Donor Advised Fund?

    Are donations on Facebook eligible for Gift Aid?
    A donation on Facebook may be eligible for Gift Aid if the person making the donation is taxpayer in the United Kingdom and the donation is being made to a UK-based nonprofit. During the donation process, a donor will be able to indicate that the donation is eligible for Gift Aid.
    Gift Aid is a tax relief program overseen by the UK government that increases the amount of a qualifying donation by 25%. For example, if a person donates £100 to a nonprofit, Gift Aid will add £25 to that donation. The nonprofit will receive a total donation amount of £125.
    If during the applicable tax year, the amount of income and/or capital gains tax paid is less than the Gift Aid amount claimed, the taxpayer will be responsible to pay the difference.
    Keep in mind that not every donation made by a UK taxpayer to a UK-based charity qualifies for Gift Aid. Learn more about Gift Aid and whether your donation qualifies for the program.
    Accessing Gift Aid Information for Your Charity
    UK-based charities can access Gift Aid transaction information in their transaction report. The report's Gift Aid UK only column will contain a 'Yes' if Gift Aid applies to the donation or 'No' if it doesn't apply.

    Reports and Receipts
    What's the difference between a Payout Report and a Daily Transaction Report?
    Charitable organizations receiving funds through Facebook Payments have access to 2 types of reports: Daily Transaction Report and Payout Report. Learn more about how to access these reports.
    Daily Transaction Report:
    • Information on each donation made to an organization, whether through the Donate button or through a fundraiser.
    • Fields like First Name, Last Name, Amount Donated and Email Address (if the donor has shared it).
    • Updated daily reports made available after 48 hours from the moment a donation was made. For example, a donation made on January 1, 2017 will appear in a report available for download after January 3, 2017.
    Payout Report:
    • What funds will be transferred into the charitable organization's account
    • Which funds are available at the end of every bi-weekly period.
    • If the organization has accrued the minimal payout amount in donations, the resulting payout occurs about 2 weeks after the end of the donation period.

    How do I view transaction reports and donor information for my charitable organization?
    If your organization receives funds through Facebook Payments, follow these steps to access transaction reports:
    1. Go to Publishing Tools on the top of your nonprofit's Page.
    2. Select Donation Settings on the left side of the Page.
    3. Click Download Donation Reports
    4. Select Payout Report or Transaction Report (reports download as .csv files).
    5. Choose time frame for report.
    6. Click Download
    Keep in mind that there's a 48-hour delay between the time a transaction occurs and the time in appears in a report.
    Daily Transaction Report Terms
    • Payment ID - The transaction/payment ID
    • Charge Time - The time the charge was accrued
    • Donation Amount - The amount donated
    • FB Fee - The processing fee charged to cover credit card processing, charity vetting, payment support and fraud prevention.
    • Net Payout Amount - The net amount donated to the nonprofit (donation amount minus FB Fee)
    • Payout Currency - Currency the donation will be paid out in
    • Sender Currency - Currency the donation was made in
    • Tax Amount - No tax will be applied
    • Charge Action Type - The type of charge made
      • S - Normal Charge
      • R - Refund
      • C - Chargeback
      • D - Out of Window Chargebacks
      • K - Chargeback Reversal
      • J - Out of Window Chargeback Reversal
      • N - Decline
    • First Name - The donor's first name
    • Last Name - The donor's last name
    • Email Address - The donor's email (if shared)
    • Campaign ID - The ID associated with that particular campaign
    • Fundraiser Title - Fundraiser title listed on Facebook
    • Source Name - Location on Facebook where the donation was made
      • donate_button_user_posts - donations raised through a user adding the donate button to their own post
      • donate_button_charity_page - donations raised through clicking donate button on the charity's Facebook Page header
      • donate_button_charity_posts - donations raised through a donate button added to a post on the charity's Facebook Page
      • video_fundraiser - donations raised from a user adding a donate button to their live video
      • fundraisers - donations raised from all other sources (example: fundraiser made by a user, Messenger or a profile frame).
    • Permalink - The link to the fundraiser on Facebook
    • Charity ID - The ID associated with the charity on Facebook
    • Campaign Owner Name - The name of the creator who made the fundraiser
    Payout Report Terms
    • Start Date (Y/M/D) - The first date of the report
    • End Date (Y/M/D) - The last date of the report
    • Net Payout Amount - The net amount paid to the charity

    Does Facebook send a payment receipt to donors?
    Yes. When someone makes a donation on Facebook to a charitable organization, a payment receipt is sent to the primary email listed on their Facebook account. This payment receipt includes the organization’s name, logo, mission statement and EIN. It also confirms that the person has made a donation as a charitable contribution and that they’re not receiving any goods or services in return.

    Fix a Problem
    An error message appears when I enter my charity's tax ID or VAT number.
    If an error message appears when you enter your charity's tax ID or VAT number while filling out the donate application, try the following tips to resolve the issue:
    • Make sure the name entered for the charitable organization matches the name on its form 990 (United States).
    • Try entering the charity's ‘DBA’ (Doing Business As) name, acronym or other name that may be on file with the IRS (United States).
    • Enter the charity's name and EIN as it appears in the GuideStar database (United States).
    • Make sure the VAT number that you provided follows the correct format (European Union).
    • Verify that you're using the correct VAT number (European Union).

    How does my charitable organization opt out of fundraisers?
    If your charitable organization has signed up for Facebook Payments, to opt out of fundraisers:
    1. Log into your Page as an admin.
    2. Click Settings at the top of your Page.
    3. Click Donations in the left column.
    4. Click to uncheck the box next to Allow people to create fundraisers.
    5. Click Save.
    Note: If you also don't want people to be able to add donate buttons to their posts about your nonprofit, click to uncheck the box next to Allow people to add donate buttons to their posts.
